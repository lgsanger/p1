Coraline and the Dragonfly Barrette

Past all its pink and blue shimmering glory, there is a much deeper symbolism behind the gemstone dragonfly barrette that Coraline adorns for the complete runtime of the 2009 stopmotion film directed by Henry Selick. While there are a number of various interpretations for the significance of dragonflies across different cultures, 3 in particular stick out to me when it comes to Coraline's characterization: Native American Traditions, Japanese symbolism, and Celtic Folklore. This barrette is a symbol of Coraline's personal and literal journey she undergoes on her adventure into the Other World and those privy to these traditions would have a hint as to how she would help the ghost children she meets along the way...
